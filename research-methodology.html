PhD Proposal

4 Research Methodology

4.1 Part 1: Establish quality measures important to sea cucumber quality evaluation.

The determination of current global quality requirements for Cucumaria frondosa will involve the use of the following resources:

  • Library literature review: books, journals, and other ad hoc materials.
  • Personal communication: with research colleagues, current industry partners, buyers, sellers.

Likely quality measures will be:

1 Organoleptic Measures: Color, texture, odor, and other appearance factors.
2 Physical Measures: Length, width, weight, shape, uniformity, thickness, elasticity and plasticity.
3 Chemical Measures: TVB, Peroxide value, TBARS, Free Fatty Acids, Enzymatic activity.

4.2 Part 2: Create quality measure analyses procedures.

Work completed in part 1 will be used to inform the work in part 2 providing recommendations for quality measurement data gathering. Part 2 will entail creating analytical procedures that will be used on Cucumaria frondosa samples that are gathered in the field and are eventually brought to a central laboratory for analysis. Quality parameter analytical procedures will be created to accomodate different infrastructure availability at each landing site. Stabilization methodologies will be developed for samples that are transported to a central laboratory for analysis.

4.3 Part 3: Create baseline quality measures for various sea cucumber handling and holding conditions.

Analytical procedures developed in Part 2 will be used to create baseline data from samples with a controlled age post harvest. Samples will be gathered from local aquaculture holding tanks and stored as per industry guidelines. Subsamples will be removed from the original sample set and quality analysis will be performed as the animals age. The rate of change of quality measures and the ballpark range of possible observed values will be determined from this work. These measures will be used as a baseline to gauge the impact of handling and holding from commercially handled sea cucumber.

4.4 Part 4: Evaluate handling performance of common fishing technologies for sea cucumber.

Over a fishing season, establish a performance expectation for current handling and holding techniques using the quality measures identified. Establish the likely causality of any degradative observations.

4.5 Part 5: Create and recommended handling practices that optimize quality measures identified.

Create improved handling practice recommendations. Implement select recommendations and establish the effect on system quality. Optimize.

Quality Measures:

Available published literature is very limited with respect to quality measurement for commercial sea cucumber (Cucumaria frondosa) harvests. Quality measures are understood at the buyer and plant production level but are largely subjective, are unpublished, and are not uniformly administered across the industry. This work will serve to create measures that can be used to both objectively and subjectively measure sea cucumber quality over its useable raw material shelf life that can standardize the resource.

Over several iterations it is proposed to:

  1. observe the changes in sea cucumber quality using current handling and holding techniques and choose key indicators of quality change from this work,
  2. construct a controlled experiment to establish the relationship (and interrelationship) between the selected measures and storage time, establishing a baseline quality relationship for the species. Where possible, current grading methodologies and criterion will be observed and recorded.

Current Production Assessment

Current harvesting processes use a modified sea urchin drag which has limited capacity per drag.
Normal trip limits are set to around 140K lb requiring several passes with the dragger on the fishing grounds to obtain. This can take often take up to 36 hours (Wade Murphy, 2018). Sea cucumber from the first drag must be stowed while the other drags are being completed. It is anticipated that there will be a difference in landed quality between the first and last drag animals which should be measured and evaluated. Recommendations can be made for handling landed raw materials based upon these observations.

Samples will be drawn from landed raw materials from each tow. These samples will be stored under controlled conditions and assessed for quality differences both initially and over 4 days. Current handling protocols will be used.
